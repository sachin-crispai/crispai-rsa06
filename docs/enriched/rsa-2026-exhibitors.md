**New Product Announcements and Key Differentiators at RSA Conference 2026**

The RSA Conference 2026 is marked by a significant emphasis on artificial intelligence (AI) and its application in cybersecurity, particularly in the realm of "agentic AI" for automated defense and threat response. Several vendors have debuted new products and capabilities, often centered around securing AI applications, leveraging AI for threat detection and response, and enhancing data security in AI-driven environments.

Key themes and differentiators emerging from the conference include:

*   **AI-Driven Security Operations & Agentic SOCs:** A major trend is the development of Security Operations Centers (SOCs) that leverage AI agents for automated investigations, threat hunting, and response. This aims to reduce costs and complexity while improving security outcomes.
*   **Securing the AI Software Supply Chain:** With the increasing adoption of AI, companies are focusing on securing the entire software supply chain for AI applications, from development to production.
*   **Zero Trust for AI Agents:** As AI agents become more prevalent, implementing Zero Trust principles to manage their access and permissions is a critical differentiator.
*   **Real-time Threat Intelligence & Preemptive Protection:** New offerings are providing real-time intelligence tailored to client needs, enabling preemptive protection against threats and exposures.
*   **Data Fabric for AI Era:** Unifying enterprise machine data to enable real-time, AI-powered threat detection and response is a key focus, breaking down data barriers while maintaining governance and security.
*   **Quantum-Ready Security:** Dell Technologies announced new quantum-ready security features within its commercial PCs, indicating a forward-looking approach to future threats.
*   **Connector Ecosystem Management:** Platforms are emerging to help cybersecurity product companies scale integrations faster and reduce the Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) for connectors, utilizing AI for validation, monitoring, and testing.

**Highlighted New Product Announcements:**

*   **Wiz AI Application Protection Platform:** Launched by Wiz (a Google-owned company), this platform aims to provide "end-to-end" security for AI applications, offering context to developer and security teams to understand cyber risks.
*   **Arctic Wolf Aurora Agentic SOC:** Arctic Wolf debuted what it calls the "world's largest agentic SOC," leveraging its new Aurora Superintelligent Platform for improved AI-driven security outcomes.
*   **Rubrik Agent Cloud with Semantic AI Governance Engine:** This technology aims to replace static, manual oversight with intent-driven governance for data security.
*   **Dataminr for Cyber Defense:** Dataminr launched an offering focused on delivering preemptive protection against threats and exposures with real-time intelligence.
*   **Cisco Zero Trust Access for AI agents:** Cisco is introducing task-based permissions and monitoring for AI agent activities to ensure strong identity and data security.
*   **Dell Technologies Quantum-Ready Security Features:** New security features within commercial PCs launching this year are designed to be quantum-ready.
*   **Sacumen ConnectX:** A unified AI platform designed to help cybersecurity product companies manage their connector ecosystems, offering prebuilt connectors, production-grade validation, agentic AI monitoring, and automated AI agent testing.
*   **Acalvio Cyber Deception Framework:** Acalvio released a new framework for cyber deception.
*   **Apiiro AI Coding Security Agent Expansion:** Apiiro is expanding the capabilities of its AI coding security agent.
*   **Bonfy Adaptive Content Security (ACS) 2.0:** A platform to monitor and control how sensitive data is accessed and handled by AI agents, copilots, and unsanctioned AI tools.
*   **Operant AI Agent ScopeGuard:** A new capability within its Agent Protector product that enforces operational boundaries for AI agents at runtime.
*   **SentinelOne AI Security Capabilities:** New AI security capabilities focusing on securing AI and using AI to automate investigations and response, including visibility and control over autonomous agents and AI red teaming.
*   **Snyk Agent Security:** A new solution designed to secure autonomous AI agents from development through production.
*   **ConductorOne AI Access Management:** A product extension providing a unified control plane for managing access to AI tools, agents, and MCP connections.
*   **CrowdStrike Platform Innovations:** Extending AI agent discovery, shadow AI governance, and runtime threat detection from the endpoint to all surfaces where AI agents operate.
*   **Arcjet Prompt Injection Protection:** A capability that inspects and blocks malicious prompts before they reach AI models, integrated with existing controls like bot detection and rate limiting.
*   **Vicarius vIntelligence:** A new flagship product adding continuous exposure validation and agentic orchestration to its vRx remediation platform.
*   **Sevii Autonomous Proactive Security (APS) module:** An expansion of its Autonomous Defense and Remediation (ADR) platform, continuously ingesting threat intelligence and deploying agentic Cyber Warrior agents.
